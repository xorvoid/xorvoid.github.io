Cycling the Pacific Coast Highway (while writing a C compiler)

Well, I had a crazy adventure this year.

I biked the entire Pacific Coast Highway (PCH) from the border with Tiujana, Mexico all the way to Vancouver, Canada. And, I wrote an entire C compiler while doing it.

While I've done many smaller bike tours in the past (weeklong, ~500 mile), this was my very first cross-continent self-supported multi-week tour.

Some numbers:

  • Distance: 2,000 miles
  • Vertical Climbing: 90,000 ft (~17 miles)
  • Time on Bike: 33 days
  • Overall Dates: 2025-05-25 to 2025-06-29
  • Average daily mileage: 61 miles
  • Average daily wind: 5-10 mph headwind
  • Weight of added gear: ~50 lbs
  • C compilers created: 1

It was a fantastic adventure packed full of unforgetable moments. And it's impossible to document them all. But, here's a small summary.

2025-06-01: Ride day 8 (Lake Nacimiento, CA => King City, CA)

Today was a day of no services (cellular, water, nor food). So I packed everything for the day.

About 10 miles outside of King City, another cyclist was heading the opposite way and crossed over to talk to me. He asked where he could get water. I said there was nothing. I gave him 1/2 a bottle and implored him to turn back. He did not...

2025-06-02: Ride day 9 (King City, CA => Marina, CA)

I really liked the Salinas Valley.

This was a required detour because Highway 1 through Big Sur seems permenantly "temporarily" closed on account of continual rock slides. It's like mother nature's way of reminding humanity that our hubris is out of control. Like... why did we build a highway there to begin with?

Anyways, the valley is beautiful and feels cozy between the two ridges. Cool to see all the argriculture. I've definitely bought food grown in these fields.

I wanted to read a John Steinbeck novel.

But I was too busy listening to Margaret Atwood.

2025-06-04: Ride day 11 (Aptos Beach, CA => Palo Alto, CA)

Today, I turned away from the coast and crossed the mountains into the Bay Area to visit a friend in Palo Alto.

It was a hard climb, followed by a super fun 4 mile, 30 mph winding descent, screaming "yeehaw cowboy!"

Surprisingly, despite being a lifelonf computer programmer, I hadn't been to Silicon Valley since ~2013 (>10 years). After all these years, I still don't understand why people flock here. Seems like an average suburb but with obscenely overpriced houses. There are quite a lot of interesting places to develop technology on this planet. It's amazing that this tiny region has convinced people otherwise.

By the way: Mountain View doesn't really have a "mountain view". Or perhaps we just have very different definitions.

2025-06-05: Rest day in Palo Alto, CA

Mostly spent the day walking around the beautiful Stanford campus and reading about its history. I like walking around engineering campuses. They have a special energy. And they give me nostalgia.

It has this really neat Christian mural, that seems very out of place at such a secular institution. Oh, how the times change...

Caught up with Eric and Jeff over dinner. We dusted off some RAGBRAI stories.

2025-06-06: Ride day 12 (Palo Alto, CA => Lagunitas, CA (Samuel Taylor State Park))

The highlight of today was definitely crossing the Golden Gate Bridge. It was a lot windier than I expected.

Stayed the night at Samuel Taylor State Park and met two Welsh bikepackers: Chris & Emily. They were biking from Crescent City to San Francisco because they just happened to be in the States for a wedding. Chris was riding with probably 75 lbs on his bike and he'd just shrug when you mentioned it. Emily was like "he's so strong it doesn't both him" and "he just casually does Iron Mans".

Again, more evidence that I'm exceedingly normal.

2025-06-09: Ride day 15 (Gualala, CA => Fort Bragg, CA)

Another beautiful day along the amazing coastline. It's brutal up-down up-down, but you see scenes like this every 5-10 miles that make you forget that you can't feel your legs:

I was dragging hard this morning. It was cold. I was beat up from the hills on the previous day.

I stopped at the Arena Market & Cafe and bumped into a guy named Aaron. He said he rode the same route from Vancouver down to Costa Rica 20 years ago. He was in area leading bike tours in Wine Country. He was also into cross-country motorcycle touring in Columbia.

That was fun. I wished him well, and set off. No matter how much you enjoy the company, you never see these "one-time friends" again. Right?

Later in the day, I stopped off at Patterson's Pub in Mendocino (Fantastic, no-nonsense pub!)

While I'm enjoying dinner, I look across and see... Aaron. What are the odds?

He's looking a lot less tired than me though...

2025-06-10: Ride day 16 (Fort Bragg, CA => Leggett, CA (Redwoods River Resort))

Today was the hardest climbing day of the entire trip. An ascent from the coastline into The Redwoods. It was a 4800ft ascent over 52 miles, including one "warm-up" climb of 1000ft that you immediately descent back to sea level before climbing another 2000ft.

Picture of a "not-broken" man...

Events

Some events:

  • I rode the first two days from Tijuana to Irvine, CA with my partner Alexandra. It was her first ever multiday tour (for 113 miles)
  • Visited my friend Kosta in Irvine
  • My bike got stolen from a hostel in Santa Barbara and I had to buy a new one at REI
  • Slept in the dried up lake bed of Lake Nacimento
  • I really liked: San Luis Obispo, Lake Nacimento, and the Salinas Valley
  • Detoured from the coast to visit my friends Eric and Jeff in Palo Alto
  • Crossed the Golden Gate Bridge, the trees are fantastic up here!
  • Slept at Samuel Taylor State Park and met two Welsh bikepackers: Chris & Emily
  • Met Aaron Frank in a random cafe. He said he rode down the PCH to Coasta Rica 20 years ago. He recruited me to join motorcyle bike trip down in Columbia.
  • Coastline north of San Fran was some of the hilliest and windiest of the entire trip. But, it was also some of the most beautiful and memorable.
  • Climbed 4800ft in 52 miles with no services (packed extra water) to get up into the Redwoods
  • Rode Avenue of the Giants
  • Everyone in Humboldt county keeps telling me how amazing their weed is and how it cures everything... Cool.
  • Ferndale, CA is a cool old school victorian-style farming town. I even joined the morning gentleman's gossip club at the general store.
  • Terifyingly dangerous section of hwy 101 between Klamath and Crescent City: (1) climbing, (2) no shoulder, (3) blind corners, and (4) 50 mph traffic
  • Met father & son bikepackers from Ireland
  • Met Alana Conner finishing her world-record ride around the world (just north of Crescent City).
  • Finally finished California. You don't understand how massive California is until you ride a bicycle across it (X days).
  • Took a ferry across the Columbia River from Oregon to Washington
  • Met a couple from Ohio on the side of the road having a "joint break". They were riding the same route in the opposite direction. We compared notes on how we "stayed alive" on roads with aggressive and entitled drivers.
  • Wore through an entire back tire and started getting flats (~1300 miles on it).
  • Stayed with Warm Showers host Beth and Pete in Olympia, WA. They rode all around the world in the 1990s. Pete replaced my back-tired and re-trued the wheel. Good as new!
  • Rode up Chuckanut to Bellingham. Its beautiful but the locals are over-obsessed with its beatuty. Or I'm spoiled by the entire Pacific Coast.
  • Crossed the border on my bike into British Columbia. It went much more smoothly than with a vehicle (crossed the border at the same location 1 year prior with a camper van).
