Writing an entire C Compiler (while cycling the PCH)

Well, I had a crazy adventure this year.

I wrote an entire C compiler from scratch including ARM64 codegen and the notorious C preprocessor. Also, I cycled up the entire PCH while doing it.

While I've written many toy compilers in the past (SectorC, Forsp, etc), this was my first full-featured mainstream-language compiler.

Some numbers:

  • Lines of code: 8322
  • Keywords recognized: 40
  • Recursive-descent parsing routines: 41
  • AST node types: 27
  • Codegen emit calls: 156
  • Testsuite assertions: 1044
  • Pacific Coastlines Cycled: 1

It was a fantastic adventure packed full of unforgetable moments. And it's impossible to document them all. But, here's a small summary.
